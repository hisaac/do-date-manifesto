The Do Date Manifesto

What Is a Do Date?

A Do Date is a time to give yourself permission to work on the things you want to work on, not on the things you have.

What you work on is up to you. Draw a picture, edit a blog post, write some code, read a book; whatever you feel is worth doing, do that.

The Tenets

  1. The goal of a Do Date is simply to do things. Nothing more, nothing less.
  2. Anything can be done at a Do Date.
  3. No one will be held accountable to complete the tasks that they plan to work on, or even work on them at all.
  4. We will enjoy each other’s company, but work hard not to disturb others while they are working.

Tips for a Successful Do Date

  • Have some easy way of notifying the members of your group that you’d like to plan a Do Date. My preferred method is email, but it could be Facebook, Twitter, text messaging; whatever works best for your group.
  • If you’d like, it can be nice to tell the group — or post on a social network — what it is you plan to work on. This could be done at a specific time, or just as people arrive.
  • The location of your Do Date is obviously up to you, but you’ll want to choose someplace easily accessible by the rest of your group, and quiet enough to concentrate. Our group usually meets in a coffee shop, but other great places are libraries, parks, or someone’s home. Groups can even meet remotely, either via some sort of video chat, or simply through a social network like Twitter.
